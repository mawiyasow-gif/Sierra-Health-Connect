HIGH SEVERITY June 2026 ⚠️ Outbreak

Malaria Peak Season Alert

Malaria transmission has increased significantly with the onset of the rainy season. All citizens are advised to take heightened preventive measures, particularly in the Western Area, Southern Province, and Eastern Province.

The Ministry of Health and Sanitation has reported a substantial increase in malaria cases across multiple districts as the rainy season intensifies. Health facilities are seeing elevated numbers of patients presenting with malaria symptoms. Children under five and pregnant women remain the most vulnerable groups. The National Malaria Control Programme is distributing insecticide-treated nets in high-burden areas.

✓ Prevention & Action Guidelines

  1. Sleep under insecticide-treated mosquito nets every night without exception
  2. Seek immediate medical attention if you experience fever, chills, or headache
  3. Pregnant women should take intermittent preventive treatment as prescribed at antenatal visits
  4. Clear standing water around your home and keep surroundings clean
MEDIUM SEVERITY June 2026 📢 Campaign

National Immunization Campaign — June 2026

The Ministry of Health and Sanitation is conducting a nationwide polio and measles vaccination campaign targeting all children under five years of age. The campaign runs from June 15-22, 2026, across all 16 districts.

Health workers will visit households, schools, markets, and community centers to administer vaccines. All children under five should receive both the polio vaccine and measles vaccine regardless of previous vaccination status. This campaign is free of charge and is part of the government's commitment to eliminating vaccine-preventable diseases. Parents and guardians are urged to ensure their children are available during the campaign period.

✓ What to do

  1. Ensure all children under five receive vaccines during the campaign period
